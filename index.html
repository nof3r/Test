What is a Haplogroup?

A haplogroup is a group of people who share a common ancestor on either the direct paternal (Y-DNA) or maternal (mtDNA) line. These groups are defined by specific mutations in your DNA that happened thousands of years ago.

Y-DNA Haplogroups

Trace your father’s father’s… father — back 200,000+ years

mtDNA Haplogroups

Trace your mother’s mother’s… mother — back to “Mitochondrial Eve”

Every human alive today belongs to one of these deep ancestral clans. Your haplogroup tells you where your ancient ancestors lived, how they migrated across continents, and which famous historical figures share your line.

No two haplogroups are the same — yours is a unique chapter in human history.
